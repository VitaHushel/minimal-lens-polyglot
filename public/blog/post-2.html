How to Choose Natural Light

Date: July 25, 2025

Natural light is a photographer's best friend when used correctly. Understanding how to work with natural light can dramatically improve your portrait photography and create stunning, professional-looking images.

Golden Hour Magic

The golden hour, occurring shortly after sunrise and before sunset, provides the most flattering natural light for portraits. The soft, warm light during this time eliminates harsh shadows and creates a beautiful glow on your subject's skin.

Window Light Techniques

Indoor window light can be incredibly versatile. Position your subject at a 45-degree angle to the window for beautiful directional lighting, or use sheer curtains to diffuse harsh sunlight for softer portraits.

Overcast Advantages

Don't overlook cloudy days! Overcast skies act as a giant natural softbox, providing even, diffused lighting that's perfect for outdoor portraits without harsh shadows.

Mastering natural light takes practice, but once you understand how to read and use available light, you'll be amazed at the professional results you can achieve.
